🚴 Recumbent Bike
A low-intensity option — good as an active warm-up before lifting, or on days when energy or joints need a break from the treadmill and elliptical.
Warm-up (10 min)
Easy resistance, comfortable pace. Use before a session when legs feel tight or you want a gradual warm-up rather than jumping straight into rowing or elliptical.
Active Recovery (20–25 min)
Moderate resistance, steady pace. Substitute for treadmill or elliptical on any session if needed — same cardio benefit, much lower joint load. Useful on rest days to keep blood moving without taxing the body.
⚡ Vibrating Plate
Not a cardio machine — but a useful recovery and activation tool. Best used after your session, before or after the sauna.
Post-session (8–10 min)
Stand on the plate after your session — feet hip-width, knees softly bent. Stimulates circulation, helps flush lactic acid, and reduces next-day soreness. Can hold a squat or calf raise position for added benefit.
Positions to try
· Standing — basic recovery, circulation
· Soft squat hold — activates glutes and quads during vibration
· Calf raises — great for lower leg recovery
· Hands on plate, plank — core activation (if comfortable)
⚠️ Avoid if you have any joint pain or dizziness. Start with the lowest setting and increase gradually. Not a substitute for cardio — it complements recovery.

Protocol: Always use after gym sessions, never before. Drink 500ml water before entering. Stop immediately if lightheaded. 3–4 sessions/week optimal for recovery and sleep.

Session A — Upper Body
SES A
Dumbbell Row
▾
How to do it
1
Place left hand and knee on bench — or stand bent over with hand on thigh
2
Hold dumbbell in right hand, arm hanging straight down
3
Drive your elbow up and back — think about pulling your elbow to the ceiling
4
Squeeze your shoulder blade at the top, hold briefly
5
Lower slowly under control — 2 seconds down
Common mistakes
✗
Twisting the torso to lift the weight — keep hips square
✗
Letting the shoulder drop at the bottom — keep shoulder packed
✗
Pulling with bicep not back — focus on the elbow, not the hand
✗
Rushing the lowering phase — the slow part builds more muscle
SES A
Dumbbell Shoulder Press
▾
How to do it
1
Sit or stand, hold dumbbells at shoulder height, palms forward, elbows at 90°
2
Press both dumbbells directly overhead until arms are fully extended
3
Lower back to shoulder height under control — 2 seconds down
4
Keep your core tight throughout — don't arch your lower back
Common mistakes
✗
Arching the lower back to press — engage core and keep ribs down
✗
Letting elbows flare too wide — keep them slightly in front of ears
✗
Pressing weight forward instead of straight up
✗
Locking out elbows aggressively at the top — keep a slight soft bend
SES A
Dumbbell Bicep Curl
▾
How to do it
1
Stand tall, hold dumbbells at sides, palms facing forward
2
Keep upper arms completely still against your sides — they must not move
3
Curl both dumbbells up towards your shoulders
4
Squeeze hard at the top for a moment
5
Lower slowly back down — 3 seconds down
Common mistakes
✗
Swinging the body to lift the weight — if you need to swing, it's too heavy
✗
Moving the upper arms forward — pin them to your sides
✗
Dropping the weight down fast — the slow lower phase is where the work happens
✗
Curling wrists at the top — keep wrists neutral
SES A
Press-ups
▾
How to do it
1
Start in high plank — hands slightly wider than shoulder width, body in a straight line
2
Lower your chest towards the floor — elbows at roughly 45° from your body
3
Your chest should nearly touch the floor
4
Press back up to full arm extension
5
Keep your body rigid throughout — no sagging hips, no raised backside
Common mistakes
✗
Letting hips sag down — your body should be one straight line
✗
Flaring elbows out at 90° — keep them at 45°
✗
Only going halfway down — full range builds more strength
✗
Head drooping — keep a neutral neck, eyes slightly ahead of hands
SES A
Plank
▾
How to do it
1
Forearms on the floor, elbows directly under shoulders
2
Rise up onto toes — body forms a straight line from head to heels
3
Brace your core as if you're about to take a punch
4
Breathe steadily — don't hold your breath
5
Hold for the target time, then lower with control
Common mistakes
✗
Letting hips drop or raise — keep that straight line
✗
Holding your breath — focus on steady breathing
✗
Looking up or dropping the head — keep neck neutral
✗
Allowing shoulders to shrug — press the floor away with your forearms
Session B — Lower Body
SES B
Goblet Squat
▾
How to do it
1
Hold one dumbbell vertically at chest height with both hands cupped under the top
2
Stand with feet shoulder-width, toes pointed out 15–30°
3
Keeping chest tall and elbows inside your knees, squat down
4
Go until thighs are parallel to the floor — or as low as comfortable
5
Drive through your heels to stand back up
Common mistakes
✗
Letting knees cave inward — push them out over your toes
✗
Rounding the back — keep chest proud throughout
✗
Heels rising off the floor — if this happens, widen stance slightly
✗
Not going deep enough — the stretch at the bottom is where you build strength
SES B
Romanian Deadlift
▾
How to do it
1
Stand holding dumbbells at thigh height, palms facing you
2
Keep a slight bend in your knees throughout — this is not a straight-leg deadlift
3
Hinge at the hips — push them back as if closing a door behind you
4
Keep the dumbbells close to your legs as you lower — almost touching the shins
5
Lower until you feel a stretch in your hamstrings, then drive hips forward to stand
Common mistakes
✗
Rounding the back — this is the most important thing to avoid
✗
Bending the knees too much — this becomes a squat, not a deadlift
✗
Letting dumbbells swing away from the legs — keep them close
✗
Going lower than your flexibility allows — stop at the stretch, not at the floor
SES B
Lateral Raises
▾
How to do it
1
Stand with dumbbells at your sides, slight bend at elbow
2
Raise both arms out to the sides simultaneously
3
Stop at shoulder height — no higher
4
Lower slowly back down — 3 full seconds
5
Lead with your elbows, not your hands
Common mistakes
✗
Raising above shoulder height — this shifts work away from the target muscle
✗
Using momentum to swing the weights up — lighter weight, strict form
✗
Shrugging the shoulders as you raise — keep them packed down
✗
Rushing the lowering phase — slow and controlled builds the shoulder
SES B
Leg Raises / Crunches
▾
How to do it
1
Lie flat on your back, arms at your sides, legs straight
2
Keeping legs straight, raise them up to 90° (pointing at the ceiling)
3
Lower slowly back down — stop just before your heels touch the floor
4
If legs straight is too hard, a slight knee bend is fine
Common mistakes
✗
Letting your lower back arch off the floor — press it flat throughout
✗
Dropping legs down fast — the slow lower is the hardest and most effective part
✗
Using momentum to swing legs up — controlled lift only
✗
Holding your breath — exhale as you raise, inhale as you lower
Cardio — Elliptical
CARDIO
Elliptical Trainer
▾
How to do it
1
Step on with feet flat on the pedals — find a comfortable stride length
2
Hold the handles lightly — the work comes from your legs, not your arms
3
Stand tall — resist the urge to lean on the machine
4
Maintain a steady pace where you can hold a conversation but it's uncomfortable
5
Push and pull the pedals in a smooth oval motion — no stomping
Common mistakes
✗
Leaning heavily on the handles — this takes load off the legs
✗
Hunching forward — stand upright throughout
✗
Taking too short a stride — use the full range of the pedal
✗
Going too slowly with no resistance — add resistance so you have to work
Session C — Whole Body (from Week 5)
SES C
Goblet Squat
▾
How to do it
1
Hold one dumbbell vertically at your chest, cupped in both hands
2
Feet shoulder-width, toes slightly out
3
Squat down as low as comfortable — thighs parallel or below
4
Drive through your heels to stand, squeezing your glutes at the top
Common mistakes
✗
Letting knees cave inward — push them out over your toes
✗
Rounding the upper back — keep the chest proud and upright
✗
Raising your heels — if this happens, widen your stance slightly
The whole-body anchor for Session C. Legs, core and upper back all working together. The dumbbell at chest forces good posture automatically.

Romanian Deadlift

Hinge at hips, pushing them back as if closing a door behind you

Dumbbell Shoulder Press
▾
How to do it
1
Seated or standing, dumbbells at shoulder height, palms forward
2
Core braced — do not arch your lower back
3
Press straight overhead to full extension
4
Lower slowly back to shoulder height — 2 full seconds down

✗
Arching the lower back to get the weight up — engage core

In Session C this ties the upper body together after the leg work. Broader shoulders improve how you look in the suit and in photos.
SES C
Dumbbell Row
▾
How to do it
1
Place one hand and same-side knee on a bench, or stand bent with hand on thigh
2
Hold dumbbell in free hand, arm hanging straight
3
Drive your elbow up and back toward the ceiling
4
Squeeze shoulder blade at the top, hold briefly
5
Lower slowly — 2 seconds down
Common mistakes
✗
Rotating your whole torso to lift the weight — keep hips square
✗
Using momentum — pull slowly and deliberately
✗
Not squeezing the shoulder blade at the top
Balances all the pressing work. Keeps the back strong and posture upright. In a whole-body session this is one of the most important exercises.
SES C
Reverse Lunge
▾
How to do it
1
Stand tall, dumbbells at your sides
2
Step one foot back — lower the back knee toward the floor
3
Front shin stays vertical — knee directly above ankle
4
Push through the front heel to return to standing
5
Alternate legs each rep
Common mistakes
✗
Letting the front knee drift forward past the toes
✗
Leaning forward at the hips — stay upright throughout
✗
Rushing — control the descent, feel the glute working
Hits glutes, quads, and core all at once. Safer on the knees than forward lunges. Adding dumbbells makes this a serious whole-body exercise.

SES C
Plank
▾
How to do it
1
Forearms on the floor, elbows under shoulders
2
Body in a straight line from head to heels
3
Brace your core as if bracing for a punch
4
Hold steady — breathe steadily throughout
Common mistakes
✗
Letting hips drop — keep everything level
✗
Holding your breath — you should be able to breathe throughout
✗
Allowing shoulders to shrug — press the floor away
The finisher for Session C. After squats, deadlifts, press and rows — the core is already warmed up. Hold for the full time.
